Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Early Life and Education

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology and later joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO).

Scientific Achievements

Dr. Kalam played a crucial role in the development of India's civilian space program and military missile systems. He was a key figure in the success of the Pokhran-II nuclear tests, showcasing India's technological prowess.

Presidency

In 2002, Dr. Abdul Kalam became the 11th President of India. His presidency was marked by his commitment to education, youth development, and promoting scientific temper among Indians.

Legacy

Even after his presidency, Dr. Kalam continued to inspire people through his speeches and writings. He was a strong advocate for education and believed in the power of youth to transform the nation.
